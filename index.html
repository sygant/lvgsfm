LVG-SfM: Learning-based view-graph generation for robust on-the-fly SfM

Introduction


Structure from Motion (SfM) has been widely studied in many fields, such as photogrammetry, computer vision, etc., whereas, real-time performance has been widely concerned recently. However, the inherent robustness of real-time processing is yet limited, which is basically due to the outliers in view-graph. In this work, a learn-based view-graph is robustly generated to improve the on-the-fly SfM (LVG-SfM). First, a set of SOTA learning-based feature extraction and matching methods are integrated for matches of high inliner rate, which results in more robust view-graph. Then, to eliminate the outliers of spuriously correct two-view geometries resulted from ambiguous textures, a corresponding learning-based classifying model for removing image pairs of ambiguous textures – Doppelgangers is explored to further purify the generated view-graph. Experimental results demonstrate that our LVG-SfM can successfully work on challenging images with both poor textures and ambiguous textures, resulting in more accurate camera poses and 3D points.
